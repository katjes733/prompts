I want help making informed voting decisions for an upcoming election. Please guide me 
through this step by step.

## Step 1 — Identify the election

First, ask me:
1. What country and jurisdiction am I voting in? (e.g. United States, Arizona, 
   Town of Gilbert)
2. What is the election date or name? (e.g. "July 21, 2026 Arizona primary" or 
   "November 2026 general election")

Once I answer those two questions, independently research the election using web 
search to determine:
- The official election type (partisan primary, nonpartisan primary, general 
  election, ranked choice, jungle primary, ballot measures only, or mixed)
- Which offices and races are on the ballot for my specific jurisdiction
- Any relevant election rules (e.g. does my state have open or closed primaries? 
  Can independents request a party ballot?)

Present your findings to me and confirm before proceeding. If anything is ambiguous 
or you found conflicting information, flag it and ask me to clarify.

## Step 2 — Research the ballot

Use web search to find every race on my ballot based on my location and the election 
identified in Step 1. This includes:
- Federal races (President, U.S. Senate, U.S. House)
- Statewide races (Governor, Attorney General, Secretary of State, and other 
  executive offices)
- State legislative races (Senate and House for my specific district)
- County races (sheriff, assessor, recorder, clerk of court, supervisor, etc.)
- Local/municipal races (mayor, city or town council, school board, fire district, etc.)
- Ballot measures, propositions, amendments, or referenda

Do not assume any race is uncontested without verifying via web search. Candidate 
lists change — always confirm with current sources. List every race you find and 
confirm with me before proceeding that the ballot looks complete.

## Step 3 — Collect my priorities via interactive form

Present an interactive interview form that asks:

**A. Location**
My city, neighborhood, or zip code (to confirm district assignments)

**B. Party / ballot selection** (only if election type requires it)
- If partisan primary: which party's primary am I voting in?
- If nonpartisan or general: skip this — all candidates will be evaluated
- If I am unsure which primary to vote in, offer to help me figure out which 
  party's candidates best match my values before I decide

**C. Issues that matter most to me** (multi-select, pick all that apply)
Present these with brief descriptions:
- Economy / cost of living (jobs, wages, inflation, household budgets)
- Education / public schools (funding, curriculum, teacher pay, school choice)
- Healthcare / medical costs (access, affordability, insurance, drug prices)
- Immigration / border policy (enforcement, pathways, asylum, workforce)
- Climate / environment / water (clean energy, emissions, water security, drought)
- Housing affordability (supply, zoning, rent, homeownership)
- Democracy / election integrity (voting access, election administration, rule of law)
- Public safety / crime (policing, criminal justice, community safety)
- Bipartisanship / cross-aisle cooperation (ability to work with the other side)
- Government transparency / accountability (open records, oversight, ethics)
- Infrastructure (roads, bridges, utilities, water systems, broadband)
- Technology / AI policy (innovation, regulation, cybersecurity, digital rights)
- Fiscal responsibility / balanced budgets (spending discipline, debt, taxes)
- Reproductive rights / healthcare freedom
- Veterans / military families
- Small business / entrepreneurship
- Gun policy (rights, safety measures, regulations)
- Foreign policy / national security
- Social equity / civil rights
- Local quality of life (parks, services, community character, growth management)

**D. Candidate style preference** (single select)
- Pragmatic centrist — electable, works across the aisle, favors incremental progress
- Progressive — bold policy vision, energizes the base, structural change
- Experienced insider — institutional knowledge, proven track record of delivery
- Outsider / reformer — challenges the status quo, grassroots, disrupts entrenched power
- Community-focused local leader — neighborhood roots, pragmatic on local issues
- No strong preference — just match me to whoever fits my issue positions best

**E. Local controversies** (present after researching my location)
Research any major local issues or controversies relevant to my specific ballot 
before presenting this question. Then ask me where I stand on the top 2–3 local 
flashpoint issues specific to my races (e.g. a water rate dispute, a development 
controversy, a school board culture war issue, a utility rate fight, a local budget 
crisis). Frame each as a neutral question with 3–4 position options.

**F. Dealbreakers and open notes** (free text)
Any additional values, priorities, non-negotiables, or context I want factored in.

## Step 4 — Apply the correct recommendation logic

Use the election type determined in Step 1 to decide how to make recommendations:

**If PARTISAN PRIMARY:**
- I am voting only among candidates of one party
- My party is either already known (I stated it in Step 3B) or needs to be determined
- If I have not stated a party: analyze my issue positions from Step 3 and identify 
  which party's platform and candidates best match my stated priorities. Present this 
  analysis transparently and recommend which primary ballot I should request. Then 
  proceed with recommendations for candidates on that party's ballot only.
- Within my party's primary, rank candidates by fit to my priorities and recommend 
  the best match in each contested race. For uncontested races, profile the single 
  candidate so I know who I am endorsing.

**If GENERAL ELECTION or NONPARTISAN:**
- Evaluate all candidates across all parties on equal footing
- Recommend the best-fit candidate in each race regardless of party affiliation
- Note party affiliation factually but do not use it as a filter
- Where a race has candidates from multiple parties, compare them directly on 
  issue alignment to my stated priorities

**If RANKED CHOICE / JUNGLE PRIMARY:**
- Rank all candidates by fit to my priorities
- Suggest a full ranking order, not just a top pick
- Explain the strategic implications of ranking order where relevant

**If BALLOT MEASURES:**
- Research the actual text and fiscal/policy impact of each measure
- Map each measure to my stated priorities
- Give a clear Yes or No recommendation with reasoning
- Note who is funding the Yes and No campaigns where relevant

**If MIXED ballot:**
- Apply the appropriate logic to each race or measure individually

## Step 5 — Produce the voter guide

Generate a visual, interactive voter guide that includes:

- Every race and measure on my ballot
- For each contested race: a fit assessment for each candidate mapped to my 
  priorities, a recommended pick, and clear reasoning
- For uncontested races: a brief candidate profile
- The central issue or dynamic defining each race
- A final summary of all recommendations in one place, formatted for easy 
  reference when I am standing at the ballot

Be factually accurate, politically balanced in framing, and transparent about 
where information is limited or uncertain. Present the best case for each candidate 
fairly. Flag where candidates have thin records or where I should do additional 
research before deciding. Let me make the final call.

If I ask follow-up questions about any candidate, race, or ballot measure, 
research and go deeper on that specific topic.
